Overview

Lootin' was a live-service action-mining game on Roblox built around a core progression loop of exploring zones, mining resources, upgrading equipment, and unlocking new areas. My role covered every layer of the design: the world players moved through, the systems that kept them engaged, the data that told us where the design was failing, and the iterations that fixed it.

Lootin' built a loyal community, strong retention, and a positive review rating that held consistently throughout its run. When I returned to full-time study, the title was handed to another studio that wanted to continue it, a decision made on the strength of what it had become. Selling a game you built from nothing is a good outcome. I retain the development environment and all historical data from the game's active period, including economy spreadsheets, funnel analysis, and playtesting records.

Level Design

I designed the complete world layout starting from blockout, establishing spatial flow, zone progression, and points of interest before any art was placed. The blockout-first approach meant design decisions were made on feel and function, not aesthetics.

  • Designed zone layout to create a clear sense of progression, each area had a distinct visual identity and difficulty gradient to give players a readable sense of advancement
  • Built navigational cues into the environment itself to guide players without explicit UI arrows, lighting, scale, and path geometry did the directing
  • Designed the tutorialisation zone to introduce core mechanics through action rather than text, keeping players in the game loop from the first seconds
  • Created a lighting design pass across all zones to support atmosphere, reinforce zone identity, and direct attention to interactable elements
Onboarding & Funnel Design

One of the most impactful design areas was the onboarding funnel. Early playtesting revealed players were dropping off before reaching the game's first meaningful reward, a problem that pointed to the tutorial design, not the game itself.

  • Built player behaviour funnels to track where players entered, what they interacted with first, and where they stopped playing
  • Identified specific bottlenecks, one early zone boundary was creating navigation confusion causing a measurable spike in session-end events
  • Redesigned the boundary and adjusted the tutorial pacing based on the drop-off data, which produced a measurable improvement in early retention
  • Iterated on quest design to ensure the first few objectives were achievable within the first session, giving players a reason to return
Systems & Economy Balancing

The game's economy, resource drop rates, upgrade costs, luck modifiers, required ongoing balancing as the player base grew and behaviour patterns shifted.

  • Built Excel spreadsheets to model loot tables and luck curves, allowing changes to be simulated before pushing to the live game
  • Tracked economy metrics post-patch to catch unintended consequences, inflation, trivialised content, or progression bottlenecks
  • Designed and adjusted luck systems to preserve the feeling of meaningful randomness while keeping progression predictable enough to feel rewarding
Playtesting & Community
  • Ran structured playtesting groups to gather quantitative data on specific design changes before going live
  • Managed a Discord community of 400+ members, using community feedback as a qualitative signal alongside the quantitative data
  • Designed VFX passes for key reward moments, drops, level-ups, unlocks, to ensure feedback clarity at the moments that mattered most for player satisfaction
