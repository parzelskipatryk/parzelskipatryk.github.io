Game Jam · Tabletop · 2–4 Players

Small Boats

Systems Design Game Jam Tabletop

A 4-player hex-tile racing board game designed and prototyped during a game jam around the theme "Small Boats." Players race across an unknown sea, managing the tension between boat size and dice advantage, betting on races with a shared economy, and exploring a tile map that reveals itself as they move.

Core Design Loop

The game is built around a single core tension: smaller boats are better at racing, larger boats are better at surviving the sea. Every strategic decision flows from this trade-off. Do you downgrade to gain dice and race aggressively? Or upgrade to tank difficult terrain and play a longer game?

That tension is supported by a betting economy and a tile-reveal exploration system, both designed to create player interaction and information asymmetry around the core loop.

Systems Design
  • Boat size vs dice pool: Each upgrade removes a die from your throw; each downgrade adds one. Larger boats gain bonuses on Deep Water tiles, smaller boats gain bonuses on Shallow Water. The system creates meaningful trade-offs at every decision point
  • Race betting: Players who enter a race place a bet, the cap is set to the lowest coin total at the table, keeping all players competitive. One player per race may raise the cap once, creating a bluffing layer. Players who don't race gain a single coin, a small but consistent alternative
  • Tile bag exploration: 80 tiles drawn blind: 40 Nothing, 15 Deep Water, 5 Shallow Water, 20 Events. The Scout action (3 chips) lets players draw and place three tiles, giving information advantage at a cost. Terrain tiles only affect players based on their boat size, so exploration value depends on your current strategy
  • Event system: 20 event cards split between one-time effects (waves, tailwinds, treasure) and permanent Perma-Events (Whirlpool, Oasis Island) that reshuffle into the deck after triggering. Perma-Events create recurring threats and opportunities that shift the board state for everyone
Win Condition Design

The dual win condition was a deliberate design decision to prevent runaway leaders and reward different playstyles across a session.

The first player to reach the end triggers a countdown, other players have a set number of rounds remaining. If multiple players finish, the winner is the player with the most gold, then smallest boat as a tiebreaker. This creates a late-game decision point: do you race to the end immediately, or hold back to accumulate coins and downgrade before crossing?

A player who raced aggressively (small boat, lots of dice, won most races) might lose to a player who played the economy and arrived last with a larger coin pool. The system rewards reading the table, not just playing optimally in isolation.

Balancing & Iteration

The Fix1 annotations in the original design document reflect balance catches made during jam playtesting. Two issues were identified and addressed before the jam ended:

  • The Thunderbolt card had too severe a penalty for small boats, the restart condition for players below size 2 was effectively eliminating players from the game rather than setting them back, breaking the comeback mechanic. The penalty was softened to a life loss and a size reduction rather than a full restart
  • Treasure coin values were too high relative to the betting economy, causing inflation in longer games and devaluing the core race mechanic. A coin gain cap was added

Both catches came directly from watching players interact with the prototype, the numbers made sense on paper but produced unintended consequences at the table.
