Relay

Level Design Puzzle Design Lighting Design VR Unity

A co-operative VR puzzle game built as a university group project. As lead designer I owned the entire level, from initial spatial blockout through to the final polished, lit environment. The central design challenge: two players sharing a physical VR space but each holding different information, requiring communication and co-operation to progress.

The Design Challenge

Asymmetric co-operation is a genuinely hard design problem in VR. Each player is physically present in the same space, they can see each other, gesture, and talk, but they're holding different pieces of the puzzle. The design had to make each player's partial knowledge feel meaningful without creating frustration when communication broke down.

There was also the VR-specific constraint of designing without a traditional HUD. No minimaps, no quest markers, no on-screen prompts. Navigation and puzzle legibility had to come entirely from the environment.

Level Design
  • Designed the complete level from scratch, starting with a spatial blockout, establishing room dimensions, flow between areas, and interaction point placement before any assets were placed
  • Designed navigational flow independently for each of the two player roles, mapping where each player would move and what they would see at each stage
  • Identified the co-operation points, the moments where players needed to combine their separate information, and designed the space around making those moments clear without being prescriptive
  • Ran iterative playtesting at the blockout stage to catch navigation failures early, before they were embedded in polished geometry
Puzzle Design
  • Designed the full puzzle flow for the session, each puzzle required both players' partial knowledge to solve, meaning neither could progress alone
  • Designed puzzles to remain legible in VR, no screen prompts, no tutorial text. All feedback came from physical interaction: sound, light, and object state changes
  • Paced the puzzle difficulty curve across the session's arc, simpler co-operation requirements early on building toward puzzles that required sustained and precise communication
  • Tested each puzzle with real pairs of players to check that the communication requirements felt natural rather than forced
Lighting Design
  • Designed the lighting pass to support atmosphere and guide player attention without explicit UI waypoints
  • Used lighting as a navigational tool, brighter areas around puzzle interaction points, cooler and dimmer ambient light elsewhere
  • Ensured lighting choices supported the mood of the experience without compromising legibility in VR headsets, where contrast can behave differently than on a monitor
