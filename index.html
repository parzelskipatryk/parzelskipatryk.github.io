About

Patryk Parzelski
Game & Level Designer, Southend-On-Sea

Design started as curiosity, and it came from Roblox. I've been playing the platform for close to 14 years and watched countless games running the exact same formula end up worlds apart, sometimes tens of thousands of players apart. That gap is what got me asking why: pulling those games apart to find whatever hook the popular one had that the near-copy didn't, even when the mechanics underneath were identical.

That same habit runs through most of my projects, pitching a change to a teammate, then working out exactly why an idea that sounded great fell apart once we broke it down. I don't trust a design decision until I've watched someone else struggle with it, and I'll rebuild a puzzle three times if a playtest says the first two weren't landing.

First Class Honours from the University of Greenwich, graduating July 2026, TIGA Graduate of the Year nominee, and currently looking for a junior design role.

Logos and wordmarks for Gramophone, Capsize, Whoever Left The Light On, and Relay are hand-drawn by me, not generated. Lootin's branding was created by our UI artist.

How I work

Principle 01

Blockout first, always.

I build every space as basic grey geometry first, no art or props. If it doesn't work in blockout it won't work polished, and problems are cheaper to fix early.

Principle 02

Test it, don't just trust it.

Every mechanic or puzzle is a guess about how players will behave. I test that guess with real players and real data instead of assuming I've got it right.

Principle 03

Document why, not just what.

My design docs explain the reasoning behind a decision, not just the decision itself, including what changed and why, so the logic is there if anyone needs to check it.

Lootin'

A Roblox action-mining game built by a four-person team, where I was the sole designer alongside a programmer, a UI artist, and an animator.   • Built luck and progression systems with Excel-based balancing models
  • Ran structured playtesting groups and analysed behavioural data

  • Managed a Discord community of 400+ active members

Perceptual Pathways

A research study investigating whether genre framing influences exploratory behaviour in identical 3D environments. Three controlled groups of 10 played the same space, told it was horror, adventure, or told nothing. All variables stripped except environment.

  • Designed a controlled environment stripping all variables except spatial layout
  • Ran a three-group participant study (n=30) with distinct genre framings
  • Analysed navigational differences between groups
  • Produced findings with implications for environmental storytelling
