About

Design that earns its decisions.

I've always been more interested in why a design works than what it looks like. That means building systems with testable assumptions, laying out levels with clear navigational logic, and not calling something finished until players prove it is.

My background spans published live-service games on Roblox, VR multiplayer puzzle design, physical puzzle installations, and research-led single-player environments. I've led design teams, run playtesting programmes, and tracked player behaviour to make decisions grounded in data rather than instinct.

Currently finishing my BSc at the University of Greenwich and leading design on Whoever Left The Light On, targeting Steam release July 2026.

How I work

Principle 01

Blockout first, always.

Every space I design starts as grey geometry. No art, no props, no atmosphere. If the level doesn't work in blockout it won't work polished — and it's cheaper to find that out early.

Principle 02

Design is a hypothesis.

Every mechanic, layout, or puzzle is an assumption about player behaviour. I test assumptions with real players and real data. Intuition is a starting point, not an endpoint.

Principle 03

Document the decisions, not just the outcome.

A design document should explain why as much as what. I write decisions down as I make them — including what changed and what prompted it — because the reasoning is half the work.
